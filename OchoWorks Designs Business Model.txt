OchoWorks Designs Business Description

House Plans
Traditional Styles
Craftsman Featuring handcrafted details, natural materials, and built-in furniture. Characterized by low-pitched roofs, exposed rafters, stone or wood siding, and covered front porches with tapered columns. Perfect for those seeking authentic American architectural heritage.
Traditional Classic home designs with timeless appeal, featuring symmetrical facades, formal room layouts, and elegant proportions. These versatile plans work well in established neighborhoods and offer enduring resale value.
Colonial Symmetrical two-story homes with center-hall entries, multi-pane windows, and simple, refined details. Often features dormers, shutters, and brick or clapboard siding. Ideal for families seeking formal living spaces.
Victorian Ornate homes with decorative trim, bay windows, turrets, and wraparound porches. Known for intricate "gingerbread" details and asymmetrical facades. Perfect for those who appreciate historical elegance.
Cape Cod Compact, efficient homes with steep roofs, central chimneys, and dormer windows. Originally designed for harsh New England weather, these cozy plans maximize space and offer charming curb appeal.
Tudor English-inspired homes featuring steep roofs, arched doorways, decorative half-timbering, and casement windows. Often includes stone or brick construction with stucco accents.
Contemporary Styles
Modern Farmhouse Contemporary farmhouse designs combining rustic charm with modern amenities. Features board-and-batten siding, metal roofs, large windows, and open floor plans while maintaining traditional farmhouse appeal.
Contemporary Clean, modern architectural designs emphasizing horizontal lines, large windows, and minimal ornamentation. Open floor plans and indoor-outdoor living integration define these forward-thinking homes.
Modern Traditional Traditional style homes updated with contemporary amenities and open floor plans. Maintains classic exterior appeal while offering modern functionality and flow.
Mid-Century Modern Post-war designs featuring flat planes, large glass windows, and open spaces. Emphasizes integration with natural surroundings and minimal interior walls.
Transitional Blends traditional and contemporary elements, offering the best of both styles. Features clean lines with some classical details, appealing to diverse tastes.
Regional & Specialty Styles
Ranch Single-story homes with horizontal emphasis, large windows, and integrated garages. Originally inspired by Western ranches, these practical designs offer single-level living convenience.
Luxury Ranch High-end single-story designs featuring premium materials, expansive layouts, and resort-style amenities. Perfect for empty nesters or those preferring elegant one-level living.
Mountain Homes Custom designs for mountainous terrain, featuring steep roofs for snow load, natural materials like stone and timber, and large windows to capture views. Often includes walkout basements.
Lakefront Properties Waterfront custom home solutions maximizing views and outdoor living. Features large decks, floor-to-ceiling windows, and layouts designed to embrace the water lifestyle.
Mediterranean Inspired by Spanish and Italian coastal architecture, featuring stucco walls, tile roofs, arched openings, and wrought iron details. Perfect for warm climates.
Prairie Style Frank Lloyd Wright-inspired designs with low horizontal lines, flat or hipped roofs, and integrated landscaping. Emphasizes harmony with the natural environment.
By Home Size & Function
Starter Homes Compact, efficient designs for first-time buyers, typically 1,200-1,800 square feet. Focus on smart space utilization and affordable construction methods.
Executive Homes Large, luxury home designs typically over 3,500 square feet, featuring high-end finishes, multiple bedrooms and bathrooms, and impressive entertaining spaces.
Custom Designs Unique architectural solutions tailored to specific client needs, lot conditions, and lifestyle requirements. Completely personalized from concept to completion.
Cottage Small, cozy homes with steep roofs, dormer windows, and charming details. Often features natural materials and efficient use of space in picturesque settings.
By Layout & Features
Single Story One-level living solutions offering convenience and accessibility. Popular with aging-in-place homeowners and those seeking easy maintenance.
Two Story Multi-level home designs maximizing square footage on smaller lots. Often features formal living areas downstairs and private bedrooms upstairs.
Open Concept Flowing, connected living spaces eliminating walls between kitchen, dining, and living areas. Perfect for modern family life and entertaining.
Basement Plans Homes featuring basement levels, adding significant living and storage space. Ideal for sloped lots or areas with deep frost lines.
Large Garages Homes with oversized garage spaces (3+ cars) or workshop areas. Perfect for car enthusiasts, hobbyists, or those needing extensive storage.
Split-Level Multi-level homes with staggered floor levels, offering separation between living areas. Popular in the 1960s-70s and making a modern comeback.
Specialty Categories
Barndominium Metal building homes combining residential living with workshop or storage space. Features open floor plans, high ceilings, and agricultural-inspired design.
Energy Efficient Homes designed to minimize energy consumption through superior insulation, efficient HVAC systems, and strategic window placement. Focus on long-term operational savings.
Smart Homes Technology-integrated designs featuring automated systems for lighting, security, climate control, and entertainment. Built for modern connected living.
Multi-Generational Homes designed for extended families, featuring separate living areas, multiple kitchens, or accessory dwelling units while maintaining family connection.
Accessible/Universal Design Homes designed for aging in place or mobility accessibility, featuring wider doorways, zero-step entries, and barrier-free design principles.
